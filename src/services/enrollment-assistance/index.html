Navigating Medicare Enrollment Doesn't Have to Be Confusing

Medicare enrollment involves strict deadlines, multiple coverage options, and complex rules that vary based on your situation. Missing a deadline or making the wrong choice can result in permanent penalties or gaps in coverage.

Our enrollment assistance service guides you through every step of the process. We help you understand which enrollment period applies to you, what forms to complete, and how to avoid the mistakes that cost many people hundreds of dollars per year in penalties.

⚠

Late Enrollment Penalties Are Permanent

If you miss your Initial Enrollment Period and don't qualify for a Special Enrollment Period, you may face lifelong penalties. The Part B penalty alone is 10% for every 12 months you were eligible but didn't enroll. These penalties never go away.

Medicare Enrollment Periods Explained

Initial Enrollment Period (IEP)

Your first chance to enroll in Medicare, lasting 7 months:

  • 3 months before you turn 65
  • Your birthday month
  • 3 months after you turn 65

Special Enrollment Period (SEP)

Available if you delayed Medicare due to:

  • Employer coverage (you or your spouse)
  • Moving to a new area
  • Losing other coverage
  • Other qualifying life events

General Enrollment Period (GEP)

If you missed your IEP and don't qualify for SEP:

  • January 1 - March 31 each year
  • Coverage starts July 1
  • Late penalties may apply

Annual Election Period (AEP)

For changing Medicare Advantage or Part D plans:

  • October 15 - December 7 each year
  • Changes take effect January 1
  • Review your coverage annually
